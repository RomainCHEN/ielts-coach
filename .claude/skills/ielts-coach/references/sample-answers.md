# Sample Model Answers for Agent Calibration

This file demonstrates the expected quality and format for each type of model answer.
The agent should match this standard when generating answers for the user.

## Sample 1: Part 1 Answer (Topic: Music, Band 7)

**User Background**: 24-year-old software engineer, learned piano as a child, enjoys K-pop

---

**Q: Do you prefer sad or happy music?**

**Model Answer:**
Generally speaking, I'd say I gravitate towards upbeat and energetic music, especially K-pop, because it really helps me unwind after a long day of coding. That said, I do appreciate a well-written ballad from time to time — there's something quite cathartic about it. But if I had to choose, happy music is definitely my go-to.

🌟 **Highlight Expressions:**
- **gravitate towards** — to be naturally attracted to something (更高级的替换 "like")
- **unwind** — to relax after stress (比 "relax" 更地道)
- **cathartic** — providing emotional release (高分情感词汇)
- **my go-to** — my preferred/default choice (地道口语表达)

💡 **Tip:** 用 "Generally speaking" 和 "That said" 自然过渡，避免机械的 "I think" 开头。

## Sample 2: Part 2 Cue Card (Band 7)

**Topic: 有趣视频 (An Interesting Video)**
**User Background**: Tech worker, interested in science, active on Bilibili

---

## Part 2: An Interesting Video

**Cue Card:**
Describe an interesting video. You should say: when and where you watched it, what it is about, why you watched it, and explain how you feel about it.

**Model Answer (~2 minutes):**

I'd like to talk about a fascinating video I came across a couple of months ago on Bilibili. It was a documentary-style piece about how artificial intelligence is transforming the healthcare industry, and I watched it late one evening in my apartment after work.

The video was roughly 20 minutes long and explored several real-world applications of AI in hospitals — things like using machine learning algorithms to detect early signs of cancer from medical scans, and how robots are assisting surgeons in performing incredibly precise operations. What really stood out to me was a segment about a startup in Shanghai that developed an AI system capable of diagnosing certain eye diseases with over 95% accuracy, which is actually on par with experienced specialists.

As someone working in the tech industry myself, I was naturally drawn to this topic. I had been reading quite a bit about AI on various tech blogs, and a colleague actually forwarded this video to me, saying it was one of the best explainers on the subject. And honestly, it didn't disappoint. What I found particularly compelling was how it struck a perfect balance — it was technical enough to be informative, yet accessible enough that someone without a tech background could follow along.

The video left a lasting impression on me for a couple of reasons. First and foremost, it opened my eyes to the tangible, life-changing impact that technology can have beyond just making apps or websites. It really drove home the point that AI isn't just some abstract concept — it's already out there saving lives. On a more personal level, it actually inspired me to explore the intersection of tech and healthcare more seriously. In fact, I'm now considering whether I could pivot my career in that direction someday. So all in all, it wasn't just an interesting watch — it genuinely shifted my perspective.

🌟 **Highlight Expressions:**
- **came across** — encountered by chance (地道表达，比 "found" 更自然)
- **what really stood out to me** — what I particularly noticed (吸引考官注意的强调句式)
- **on par with** — equal in standard to (高分比较表达)
- **naturally drawn to** — instinctively attracted to (自然兴趣表达)
- **struck a perfect balance** — achieved the ideal combination (高分搭配)
- **left a lasting impression on me** — had a deep impact (替代 " impressed me")
- **drove home the point** — made something very clear (地道习语)
- **pivot my career** — change career direction (商务英语词汇)
- **shifted my perspective** — changed my way of thinking (高分替换 "changed my mind")

📝 **Structure Notes:**
- Introduction: Sets the scene (what + when + where + what video was about)
- Body: Detailed description + personal connection as a tech worker
- Conclusion: Impact on the speaker — both intellectual and personal, wraps up with forward-looking statement

## Sample 3: Part 3 Answer (Band 7)

**Q: What makes a video go viral online?**

**Model Answer:**
That's an interesting question. I think there are several factors at play. To begin with, emotional resonance is probably the single most important ingredient — videos that make people laugh uncontrollably, feel deeply moved, or even get genuinely angry tend to spread like wildfire because people feel compelled to share those strong emotions with others. On top of that, timing plays a crucial role. A video that taps into a current trend or a hot topic has a much higher chance of going viral than one that doesn't. I've also noticed that shorter videos, say under 60 seconds, tend to perform better simply because people's attention spans are getting shorter these days. But ultimately, I'd say there's a certain element of unpredictability — you can't really manufacture virality; sometimes something just catches on for reasons nobody could have predicted.

🌟 **Highlight Expressions:**
- **several factors at play** — multiple factors involved (学术表达)
- **emotional resonance** — emotional connection (高分词汇)
- **spread like wildfire** — spread very quickly (地道习语)
- **taps into** — makes use of / connects with (地道短语)
- **attention spans** — ability to concentrate (话题词汇)
- **manufacture virality** — artificially create viral success (搭配亮点)

## Sample 4: Writing Task 2 Essay (Band 7)

**Question**: Some people think that governments should spend more money on public transport rather than building new roads. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

**User Background**: Urban resident, commutes daily, environmentally conscious

---

**Model Essay (285 words):**

In recent years, the debate over how governments should allocate transportation budgets has become increasingly prominent. While some argue that constructing new roads should take priority, I firmly believe that investing in public transport is the more sensible approach for the vast majority of cities.

First and foremost, an efficient public transport system can significantly alleviate traffic congestion, which is a pressing issue in virtually every major city. When buses, subways, and light rail systems are reliable, affordable, and well-connected, people are far more inclined to leave their cars at home. Take Shanghai as an example — the city's extensive metro network carries over 10 million passengers daily, which dramatically reduces the number of private vehicles on the road. Without this infrastructure, the city would quite literally grind to a halt.

Furthermore, expanding public transport is undeniably more environmentally sustainable than building additional roads. Road construction not only consumes vast amounts of concrete and asphalt — materials with substantial carbon footprints — but also tends to induce demand, meaning that new roads quickly fill up with more cars, creating a vicious cycle. In contrast, a single full bus can replace up to 40 private vehicles, and electric-powered light rail produces virtually zero emissions at street level. If governments are serious about meeting their climate targets, prioritizing public transport over roads is a logical first step.

Of course, there is a counter-argument that roads remain essential for freight transport, emergency vehicles, and connecting rural areas where public transport may not be economically viable. This is a valid point, and I am not suggesting that road funding should be eliminated entirely. However, in terms of where the bulk of additional investment should go, public transport clearly offers greater long-term benefits for the majority of urban populations.

In conclusion, while road infrastructure should not be entirely neglected, I am strongly of the opinion that public transport deserves the lion's share of government transportation budgets. The advantages in terms of congestion relief, environmental protection, and overall quality of life in cities are simply too significant to ignore.

🌟 **Highlight Expressions:**
- **allocate budgets** — assign money (学术搭配)
- **alleviate traffic congestion** — reduce traffic (高分学术词汇)
- **pressing issue** — urgent problem (强调严重性)
- **grind to a halt** — stop completely (地道习语)
- **induce demand** — create more demand (经济学概念词汇)
- **vicious cycle** — negative feedback loop (地道表达)
- **the lion's share** — the largest part (地道习语)

📝 **Structure Notes:**
- Introduction: Context + clear position
- BP1: Congestion argument with Shanghai example
- BP2: Environmental argument with specific data comparisons
- BP3: Concession to balance the argument
- Conclusion: Restates position with summary of reasons
