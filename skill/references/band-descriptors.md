# IELTS Speaking Band Descriptors Reference

This reference ensures model answers consistently match the user's target band score.

## Speaking Band Descriptors Summary

### Band 6 — Competent User
| Criterion | Characteristics |
|-----------|----------------|
| Fluency & Coherence | Willing to speak at length, though may lose coherence at times. Uses a range of connectives but not always appropriately. |
| Lexical Resource | Has enough vocabulary to discuss topics at length. Generally paraphrases successfully. Uses some less common vocabulary with mixed success. |
| Grammatical Range | Uses a mix of simple and complex structures, but with limited flexibility. May make frequent mistakes with complex structures. |
| Pronunciation | Uses a range of pronunciation features with mixed control. Can generally be understood throughout. |

**Target: ~2-4 sentences per Part 1 answer, ~200 words for Part 2**

### Band 7 — Good User
| Criterion | Characteristics |
|-----------|----------------|
| Fluency & Coherence | Speaks at length without noticeable effort. Uses a range of connectives and discourse markers with some flexibility. |
| Lexical Resource | Uses vocabulary resource flexibly to discuss a variety of topics. Uses some less common and idiomatic vocabulary. Shows awareness of style and collocation. |
| Grammatical Range | Uses a range of complex structures with some flexibility. Frequently produces error-free sentences, though some grammatical mistakes persist. |
| Pronunciation | Shows all the positive features of Band 6, and some (but not all) of Band 8. |

**Target: ~3-4 sentences per Part 1 answer, ~250 words for Part 2**

### Band 8 — Very Good User
| Criterion | Characteristics |
|-----------|----------------|
| Fluency & Coherence | Speaks fluently with only occasional repetition or self-correction. Develops topics coherently and appropriately. |
| Lexical Resource | Wide vocabulary resource readily and flexibly. Skillful use of less common and idiomatic vocabulary. Occasional inaccuracies in word choice and collocation. |
| Grammatical Range | Wide range of structures flexibly and accurately. Majority of sentences error-free. Only very occasional inappropriacies or basic errors. |
| Pronunciation | Uses a wide range of pronunciation features. Sustains flexible use of features. Easy to understand; L1 accent has minimal effect on intelligibility. |

**Target: ~3-5 sentences per Part 1 answer, ~280 words for Part 2**

## Writing Band Descriptors Summary

### Task Response
- **Band 6**: Addresses all parts of the task though some parts may be more fully covered. Presents a relevant position, though conclusions may become unclear.
- **Band 7**: Addresses all parts of the task. Presents a clear position throughout. Supports main ideas but may over-generalize.
- **Band 8**: Sufficiently addresses all parts. Well-developed response with extended and supported ideas.

### Coherence & Cohesion
- **Band 6**: Arranges information coherently. Uses cohesive devices effectively but cohesion within/between sentences may be faulty.
- **Band 7**: Logically organizes information. Clear progression throughout. Uses a range of cohesive devices appropriately.
- **Band 8**: Sequences information and ideas logically. Manages all aspects of cohesion well. Uses paragraphing sufficiently and appropriately.

### Lexical Resource
- **Band 6**: Adequate range of vocabulary. Attempts less common vocabulary with some inaccuracy. Some errors in spelling/word formation.
- **Band 7**: Sufficient range to allow flexibility. Uses less common lexical items with some awareness. Occasional errors in word choice.
- **Band 8**: Wide range of vocabulary fluently and flexibly. Skillful use of uncommon items. Rare spelling/word formation errors.

### Grammatical Range & Accuracy
- **Band 6**: Mix of simple and complex forms. Some errors when attempting complex sentences.
- **Band 7**: Variety of complex structures. Frequent error-free sentences. Good control but a few errors.
- **Band 8**: Wide range of structures. Majority error-free. Only occasional errors.

## Practical Guidelines for Model Answers

### Vocabulary Density by Band

| Level | Less Common Words per Part 2 Answer | Idiomatic Expressions | Topic-Specific Vocabulary |
|-------|-------------------------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Band 6 | 3-5 | 0-1 | Basic range |
| Band 7 | 6-10 | 1-3 | Good range |
| Band 8 | 10-15 | 3-5 | Wide, nuanced range |

### Cohesive Devices by Band

| Level | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Band 6 | and, but, because, so, also, for example |
| Band 7 | furthermore, moreover, in addition, consequently, on the other hand, in contrast, as a result, in terms of |
| Band 8 | having said that, be that as it may, by the same token, in light of, notwithstanding, it is worth noting that, arguably |

### Sentence Complexity by Band

| Level | Characteristic |
|-------|---------------|
| Band 6 | Simple + occasional complex. 1 dependent clause max per complex sentence. |
| Band 7 | Regular complex sentences. Mix of clause types (relative, conditional, adverbial). |
| Band 8 | Sophisticated embedding. Multiple clause types in one sentence. Natural variation. |
